    <h2 class="arabic-text">1️⃣ - Iriomote Cat – Full Profile</h2>

    <p class="english-text"><strong>🐱 What is the Iriomote Cat?</strong><br>
    The Iriomote cat is a rare and endangered wild cat found only on Iriomote Island in Japan.</p>

    <h2 class="arabic-text">2️⃣ - Physical Description</h2>
    <ul class="english-text">
      <li>Size: 45–55 cm (body), 25–30 cm (tail)</li>
      <li>Weight: 2.0 to 3.5 kg</li>
      <li>Fur: Reddish-brown with spots</li>
      <li>Eyes: Large and night-adapted</li>
      <li>Tail: Bushy for balance</li>
    </ul>

    <h2 class="arabic-text">3️⃣ - Habitat and Location</h2>
    <ul class="english-text">
      <li>Found only on Iriomote Island (~289 km²)</li>
      <li>Prefers rainforests, mangroves, wetlands</li>
    </ul>

    <h2 class="arabic-text">4️⃣ - Classification</h2>
    <ul class="english-text">
      <li>Family: Felidae</li>
      <li>Genus: Prionailurus</li>
      <li>Species: bengalensis</li>
      <li>Subspecies: iriomotensis</li>
    </ul>

    <h2 class="arabic-text">5️⃣ - Why Is It Endangered?</h2>
    <ul class="english-text">
      <li>Habitat loss</li>
      <li>Roadkill</li>
      <li>Invasive species</li>
      <li>Pollution</li>
      <li>Low genetic diversity</li>
    </ul>

    <h2 class="arabic-text">6️⃣ - How to Protect It</h2>
    <ul class="english-text">
      <li>Protected areas</li>
      <li>Wildlife crossings</li>
      <li>Control stray animals</li>
      <li>Eco-tourism awareness</li>
      <li>Support research</li>
    </ul>

    <h2 class="arabic-text">7️⃣ - Related Species</h2>
    <p class="english-text">
      Some scientists debate whether it's a unique subspecies or a local population of the leopard cat. Still, it's genetically and behaviorally distinct.
    </p>

    <h2 class="arabic-text">8️⃣ - Summary</h2>
    <ul class="english-text">
      <li><strong>Name:</strong> Iriomote cat</li>
      <li><strong>Status:</strong> Critically Endangered</li>
      <li><strong>Location:</strong> Iriomote Island, Japan</li>
      <li><strong>Population:</strong> Fewer than 100 in the wild</li>
      <li><strong>Main Threats:</strong> Habitat destruction, road accidents, invasive species</li>
      <li><strong>Protection:</strong> Conservation zones, education, safety, monitoring</li>
    </ul>

    <h3 class="arabic-text">🌙 Daily Activity Pattern</h3>
    <ul class="english-text">
      <li><strong>Active at night (nocturnal)</strong>, especially during dusk and dawn.</li>
      <li><strong>Males:</strong> increased movement during mating season.</li>
      <li><strong>Females:</strong> slight changes in behavior during nursing.</li>
    </ul>
    <p class="english-text"><strong>📚 Source:</strong> SpringerLink Study on Behavioral Patterns</p>

    <h3 class="arabic-text">🏞️ Territorial Behavior</h3>
    <ul class="english-text">
      <li><strong>Females:</strong> maintain separate, non-overlapping territories.</li>
      <li><strong>Males:</strong> wider territories that may overlap with females.</li>
      <li>Use scent marking to define and defend territory, mostly at night.</li>
    </ul>
    <p class="english-text"><strong>📚 Source:</strong> JTEF Conservation Info</p>

    <h3 class="arabic-text">🍴 Diet and Hunting</h3>
    <ul class="english-text">
      <li>Small mammals (bats, rodents)</li>
      <li>Birds, reptiles, amphibians</li>
      <li>Insects, fish, and freshwater crabs</li>
    </ul>

    <h3 class="arabic-text">🧬 Reproduction and Lifecycle</h3>
    <ul class="english-text">
      <li><strong>Breeding:</strong> Winter to early spring</li>
      <li><strong>Birth:</strong> Spring (~60–70 days gestation)</li>
      <li><strong>Litter Size:</strong> 1–2 kittens</li>
      <li><strong>Maternal Care:</strong> Hidden dens, high care level, exploration starts after months</li>
      <li><strong>Activity:</strong> Females increase daytime movement when raising cubs</li>
    </ul>
